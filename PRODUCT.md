# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pâtiss'App is used by bakery and pastry teams during daily operations: managers watching sales and stock, sellers taking orders and payments, pastry staff preparing production, and SaaS super-admins supporting client organizations. The common context is a busy shop or lab, often on tablet or mobile, with interruptions, customers waiting, and unreliable network conditions.

## Product Purpose

The product centralizes checkout, custom orders, product catalogue, stock, team management, reporting, and AI-assisted financial insight for independent bakeries and pastry shops. Success means staff can complete core tasks quickly, recover from offline moments, trust the numbers, and understand what needs attention without reading a manual.

## Brand Personality

Warm, precise, and dependable. The interface should feel like a professional workbench for artisans: tactile enough for touch use, calm enough for repeated daily workflows, and refined enough to support a premium food business without becoming decorative.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a generic SaaS dashboard, a cold supermarket POS, a marketing landing page, or a heavily decorative bakery moodboard. Avoid gimmicky gradients, over-rounded cards, low-contrast beige-on-beige text, decorative motion, and layouts that prioritize visual flourish over speed.

## Design Principles

1. Put the transaction first. Selling, creating an order, and checking stock must stay faster than the visual system around them.
2. Make status impossible to miss. Payment, production, stock, network, and subscription states should be clear without relying on color alone.
3. Keep warmth in service of clarity. The bakery identity can appear through color, language, and tactility, but never at the cost of contrast or density.
4. Design for hands, not just cursors. Touch targets, numeric entry, modals, and bottom navigation should support tablet workflows during service.
5. Preserve trust. Multi-tenant data, offline sync, and AI outputs must look and behave as controlled product surfaces, not as loose experiments.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA as the default standard: body text contrast at 4.5:1 or better, visible keyboard focus, clear error messages, touch targets around 44px or larger, reduced-motion fallbacks, and status labels that do not rely on color alone.
